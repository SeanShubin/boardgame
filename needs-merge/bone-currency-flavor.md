# Bone Currency — Flavor Text

Flavor text for the **Bone** currency (the Controller role's currency in Deckbound),
written to establish that currency-bone is distinct from ordinary bone.

## The core distinction

> **Death makes bone, but only terror makes money.**

Raw bone is the byproduct of *dying* — abundant, white, brittle, worthless. Currency-bone
is the residue of a *will breaking* (a Fear-break, not a Body death): cured dark, struck with
a mark, bound so it can't be faked and can't forget whose it was. When writing more flavor in
voice, lean on **remembers / afraid / marked / taken-not-given** to stay consistent.

---

## Options by surface

### Rulebook sidebar — "Why bone is money"
*(teaches the distinction while it flavors)*

> A corpse is not coin. The dead of the sword and the cannon leave only raw bone — white,
> brittle, chalk for the road. Currency is crueler than that. When a thing breaks not in body
> but in *will*, the terror sets into the marrow and will not leave. Cure that bone dark, strike
> it with the mark, and it rings like fired clay and never forgets whose it was. Carry a fistful
> and you carry a fistful of broken enemies — which is why no one refuses it, and why none will
> meet your eye while you count.

### Codex epigraph — short, for a chapter head or splash

> *Any grave is full of bone. Only the bone that remembers the moment a will gave way is worth
> a thing — and that kind is never given freely. It is taken.*

### Merchant's voice — for a vendor screen or card

> "You brought me a sack of bone." The bonewright didn't look up. "Cow, dog, a man dead in his
> sleep — all the same. Chalk. I deal in the *other* kind." He turned one disc to the light, dark
> and marked. "The kind that's still afraid."

### Card-back / tooltip — terse

> **Bone.** Not the kind left by death — the kind left by *terror*. Cured, marked, and unwilling
> to forget.

### Contrast couplet — crisp, two lines

> Bone is cheap; every grave is full of it.
> A bone-mark is dear; it had to be frightened out of someone.

---

## Background (design rationale, for the merger)

The distinction rests on a Body/will split the combat model already has: a foe killed in **Body**
yields raw, worthless bone; a foe broken in **will** (Fear damage past its **Resolve** — a
will-break) yields bone that "remembers" and can be consecrated/marked into spendable currency.
This makes Bone *the Controller's* currency at the supply level (the Controller is the one who
breaks wills) and turns the dread theme into the coin's anti-counterfeit provenance.
