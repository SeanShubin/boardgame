# Deckbound ‚Äî Sample Combat (4 vs 5)

A card-level play-by-play of one round on the **current** design: all four aspects
(Body / Mind / Magic / Spirit), the **gauntlet**, **fear vs resolve**, **reads +
momentum + combos**, **conditional decks**, and the **capability-card + rules-card**
model (plain tokens flip Form‚ÜîDormant; a rules card holds the magnitude; damage
**accumulates within a round** as cards in a zone). Rebalanced toward the
**one-reviver margin** ‚Äî a real fight that should *cost* a careful party, not a
curbstomp.

Zone shorthand: `Strike: P‚ÜíA‚ÜíD` (aggressive ‚Üí exhausts); `Block: P‚ÜíA‚ÜíP` (defensive ‚Üí
self-returns); `Firestorm: P‚ÜíA‚ÜíD` (Fleeting); `+Spd ‚ÜíA` (momentum banked); `Body √ó2:
Form‚ÜíD` (cards flipped down).

## Form up & declare

- **Players** ‚Äî Front: Aldric, Vera. Back: Sefa, Bram.
- **Creatures** ‚Äî Front: Ogre. Diving: Stalker + 2 Imps. The **Wraith**, incorporeal,
  drifts past the wall to **haunt Sefa**.
- A reasonable plan: **Aldric Holds**, **Vera Attacks the Ogre**, **Bram Rallies
  Sefa**, **Sefa casts Firestorm**. (Note: only *one* Holder covers the gauntlet.)

## Resolve in Speed order

**Stalker (Spd 6) ‚Äî dive ‚Üí gauntlet.** Runs past Aldric (lone Holder, wall speed = his
Spd 3). 3 < 6, so it **slips the block**; Aldric's **free strike auto-lands** anyway
(`Bash: Pow 4 ‚Üí Body √ó4: Form‚ÜíD`). But the Stalker is **Body 6** now ‚Äî it survives at
**2**, **bloodied but through**, and reaches Sefa. *One Holder is a thin gauntlet: fast
**and** tough enough, a diver breaks it.*

**Stalker ‚Üí Sefa.** Sefa declared **Attack** (casting), not Hold ‚Äî so she is **not
engaging** the Stalker, and its strike **auto-succeeds**: `Pow 3 ‚Üí Body √ó3: Form‚ÜíD` ‚Üí
**Sefa knocked out** before she can cast. Firestorm never goes off. **Bram's passive
revive** catches her ‚Äî she's back up, but her turn is **spent on the floor**.

**Imps (Spd 4) ‚Äî gauntlet.** Both run past Aldric; free strikes (Pow 4 ‚â´ Body 1) ‚Üí
**both dead**. The wall handles the *weak* divers cleanly.

**Wraith (Spd 4) ‚Äî haunt Sefa.** Its prey is already down ‚Äî nothing to frighten ‚Äî so it
**recoils**, Presence intact. (Bram's Rally, aimed at the Wraith's fear, was the *wrong
threat*: it was a **blade**, not dread, that dropped Sefa.)

**Vera (Spd 5) ‚Äî read the Ogre.** Ogre telegraphs **Press**; Vera **Ripostes** (`Evade:
P‚ÜíA‚ÜíP`, **+Spd ‚ÜíA**) ‚Äî negates the swing, takes position, but sharp 3 ‚àí armor 3 = **0**,
no wound. She's **banking momentum** toward a blow big enough to crack the armor.

**Aldric (Spd 3) ‚Äî Hold.** Free strikes already landed; he braces, untouched.

**Bram (Spd 3) ‚Äî Rally** (`P‚ÜíA‚ÜíD`) ‚Äî resolved early, and his **passive revive** already
caught Sefa. A turn spent without stopping the real killer.

**Sefa (Spd 2)** ‚Äî down; no action.

**Ogre (Spd 2)** ‚Äî its Press at Vera was **Evaded**; wasted.

## End of round

| | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| **Heroes** | **Sefa dropped and revived** (no Firestorm; she contributed nothing). Others unhurt; Vera banked **+Spd**. **No permanent loss ‚Äî the one-reviver margin held.** |
| **Creatures** | **2 of 5 down** (the Imps). **Stalker** survives at Body 2 in the back line (next round, alone ‚Üí it flees). **Wraith** recoiled, Presence 3. **Ogre** untouched at 8 (no Firestorm landed; Vera's sharp can't bite it). |

This is what **balanced** feels like: a reasonable plan, and the warband still **made
them bleed** ‚Äî a hero down, the Mage's whole turn lost, two real threats (Ogre, Wraith)
standing. They held only because Aldric walled the imps and **Bram's revive caught
Sefa**. The lesson is legible: against a **tough, fast diver**, one Holder is not
enough ‚Äî had **Vera also Held**, the two-body gauntlet (wall speed 3 **+1** = 4, and a
second free strike) would have **killed the Stalker on the run** and freed Sefa to
cast. The fix is coverage; the cost of greed is a hero on the floor.

**Careless** (no Holders at all) is a flat wipe: Stalker + Imps gut the back line, the
Wraith scares whoever's left, and nobody is up to revive.

## What it exercises ‚Äî and what's now settled

In play: all four aspects, the **gauntlet** (coverage as *speed pooled by bodies* ‚Äî a
lone Holder couldn't keep up), **fear vs resolve** (the Wraith bypassing the wall),
**momentum** vs armor, the **capability/rules-card** model, **stance vs read** (Sefa's
Attack left her undefended), and **revive as the margin**.

- **Resolve** ‚Äî *settled:* a stat (cards + rules card), **fear accumulates within a
  round, resets between**; overcome it and your own panic harms you. See
  [spirit](spirit.md).
- **Incorporeal** ‚Äî *settled:* drifts past the gauntlet, stopped only by resolve; only
  **Dread** harms it ([form & defeat](form-and-defeat.md#incorporeal--no-body-no-shelter-for-the-soul)).

Still open (numbers / grammar):

- **Combo grammar** ‚Äî how a card like *Riposte* bundles Evade + counter + reposition
  (see [combos](combos.md)).
- The exact **scaling** so a balanced fight reliably lands on the one-reviver margin.
