# Deckbound — Combat Resolution

How a committed exchange resolves into **magnitude**. Once the reads
([the Mind](mind-and-reads.md)) and the cards
([Body stats & deliveries](cards-and-customization.md)) are chosen, this is the math
that turns them into an outcome. Numbers here are deferred until there is something to
playtest.

## Order and interruption

- **Speed sets the order** — faster actions resolve first (Speed 0 = motionless).
- **Power resists interruption** — a higher-Power action is harder to interrupt; a
  fast, strong strike can cancel a slower or fragile one (notably a Scheme).
- The full **interrupt rule** — Speed to arrive in time, Power to not be ignored —
  and front-line interception live in
  [coordination & interruption](coordination-and-interruption.md).

## From a read to damage

A landed Strike's damage is built from the Body axes plus the
[Mind's banked momentum](mind-and-reads.md#momentum--the-thrill): **Power** (and any
attached multipliers) sets force; **Precision** exploits weak spots and bypasses
armor. That damage is **typed**, then erodes the target's **Body** capability cards
through their armor and toughness — see
[form & defeat](form-and-defeat.md#how-damage-resolves).

Mirror and trade matchups in the [read cycle](mind-and-reads.md#the-cycle) (Strike vs
Strike, Block vs Evade, …) are settled here, by Speed order and Power.
