# Treasure Dive

A small, original push-your-luck card game for 2 or more players. Press your
luck diving for treasure — but flip one suit too many and you surface with
nothing.

## Components

A single deck of **36 cards**: six suits — Anchor, Fish, Chest, Map, Sword,
Pearl — each with one card of every value from **1 to 6**.

## Setup

Shuffle the deck into a face-down draw pile. Each player starts with a score of
**0**. Choose a first player; play proceeds in seat order.

## Your turn

On your turn you build a **dive pile** in front of you, one card at a time. You
have two choices, and you keep choosing until your turn ends:

- **Dive** — flip the top card of the draw pile onto your dive pile.
  - If its suit is **new** to your dive pile, it stays. You may dive again or
    surface.
  - If its suit **already appears** in your dive pile, you **bust**: your entire
    dive pile (and the card that busted it) is discarded, you score nothing this
    turn, and your turn ends.
- **Surface** — stop diving and **bank** your dive pile: add the total value of
  its cards to your score. Your turn ends.

Then play passes to the next player.

## Ending the game

When the draw pile runs out, the active player banks whatever is in their dive
pile and the game ends immediately.

The player with the **highest score wins**. If several players are tied for the
highest score, they share the win.

## Strategy in a sentence

Every successful dive adds value but also adds a suit, so each further dive is
riskier than the last — bank a good pile before greed busts it.
